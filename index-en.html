Second Place in a Hackathon

University of Córdoba 2nd place

I participated in a university hackathon in collaboration with IMIBIC, developing, together with my team, a software solution in an intensive and collaborative work environment.

The challenge consisted of finding a solution to determine whether a person is easy or difficult to intubate, since healthcare professionals need this information but false positives can occur. To address this, we developed a neural network on a Raspberry Pi 5 Model B that, through a camera, captured an image of the patient’s face and, using a machine learning model, determined whether the patient was easy or difficult to intubate.

I participated in the frontend development with PyQT5, the hardware architecture and the integration of the machine learning model into the application. This experience allowed me to apply my technical knowledge in a real project, work under pressure as part of a team and develop communication and presentation skills.

The prototype was called "IntubIA" and, thanks to the MediaPipe library, it achieved second place in the competition, receiving recognition for its innovation and applicability in the healthcare field.
